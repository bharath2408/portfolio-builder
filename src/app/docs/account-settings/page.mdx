# Account Settings

The Settings page is your control center for managing your profile, security, preferences, and data. Access it from the sidebar: **Account > Settings**.

## Profile

### Profile Picture

Upload a profile picture that appears in the navigation bar and across the platform.

1. In the Profile section, click the **Upload** button next to your avatar
2. Select an image from your computer (or drag and drop)
3. The image uploads to Cloudinary automatically
4. Click **Save Changes** to persist the new profile picture

**Supported formats:** PNG, JPG, GIF, WebP
**Maximum file size:** 10MB
**Display:** Circular crop, shown at various sizes across the app

To remove your profile picture, hover over the thumbnail and click the **X** button, then save.

> **Note:** After saving, the navbar avatar updates to show your new profile picture instead of your initials.

### Full Name

Your display name shown across your portfolios and the navigation bar.

- **Minimum:** 2 characters
- **Maximum:** 50 characters
- Click **Save Changes** after editing

### Username

Your unique identifier that determines your portfolio URL. For example, username `alexchen` makes your portfolio available at `/portfolio/alexchen`.

- **Minimum:** 3 characters
- **Maximum:** 30 characters
- **Allowed characters:** lowercase letters (a–z), numbers (0–9), hyphens (`-`), underscores (`_`)
- Must be unique — if taken, you'll see an error when saving
- The URL preview updates as you type

### Bio

A short description about yourself displayed on your profile.

- **Maximum:** 500 characters
- Supports plain text only
- Good for a one-liner about your role, expertise, or interests

## Appearance

### Dashboard Theme

Switch the dashboard interface between **Light** and **Dark** mode. This only affects the dashboard and editor — not your published portfolios.

1. Click the **Light** or **Dark** theme card
2. The theme applies immediately — no need to save
3. Your preference persists across sessions

The theme cards show a miniature preview of what each mode looks like.

> **Note:** Your portfolio themes are set independently per-portfolio in the Studio Editor's theme panel.

## Account

### Email
Your account email address. This is read-only and shows a **Verified** badge. The email is used for login (if using email/password authentication) and notifications.

### Role
Your account role (**User** or **Admin**). This determines your access level within the platform.

### Your Site URL
If you have a username and a custom domain is configured, your personal site URL is displayed here with a copy button.

### Change Password

For accounts created with email and password:

1. Click the **Change Password** button in the Account section
2. The password form expands with three fields:
   - **Current Password** — enter your existing password (eye icon to show/hide)
   - **New Password** — enter your new password (eye icon to show/hide)
   - **Confirm Password** — re-enter the new password
3. Password requirements: 8+ characters, at least one uppercase, one lowercase, one number
4. Click **Update Password**
5. A success message confirms the change

If your password is incorrect, an error message appears. You can click **Cancel** to close the form.

> **Note:** Accounts created via GitHub or Google login don't have a password set. The Change Password button is disabled with a message explaining that you use social login.

## Preferences

### Email Notifications

Toggle email notifications on or off with the switch:

- **Enabled** (default) — receive emails about portfolio views, contact form submissions, and tips
- **Disabled** — no notification emails

The change saves immediately when you toggle — no need to click Save Changes.

### Default Portfolio

Choose which portfolio is displayed when someone visits your username URL (`/portfolio/your-username`):

1. Select a portfolio from the dropdown
2. The change saves immediately
3. The selected portfolio gets an `isDefault` flag

This dropdown only appears if you have at least one portfolio. If you have no portfolios, create one first.

> **Tip:** Set your best or most complete portfolio as the default — it's the first thing people see when they visit your profile URL.

## Data

### Export All Data

Download a complete JSON export of all your data:

1. Click the **Export JSON** button
2. A file downloads named `foliocraft-export-YYYY-MM-DD.json`
3. The file contains:
   - Your profile information (name, email, username, bio, settings)
   - All portfolios with complete data:
     - Sections (names, layout, styles)
     - Blocks (type, content, styles)
     - Theme settings
   - Timestamps for all records

The export is useful for:
- **Backups** — save your data locally before major changes
- **Migration** — move your data to another platform
- **Analysis** — examine your portfolio structure programmatically

## Danger Zone

### Delete Account

Permanently and irreversibly delete your entire Foliocraft account:

1. Scroll to the **Danger Zone** section (red border)
2. Click **Delete My Account**
3. A confirmation panel expands
4. Type exactly `delete my account` in the text field
5. The **Permanently Delete** button activates
6. Click it to proceed
7. Your account is deleted and you're signed out

### What Gets Deleted

Everything associated with your account is permanently removed:

- Your profile (name, email, username, bio, avatar)
- All portfolios and their content
- All sections and blocks
- All themes and custom styles
- All analytics data and view counts
- All contact form submissions
- All version history snapshots
- All linked OAuth accounts (GitHub, Google)

> **Warning:** This action is completely irreversible. There is no recovery process. Export your data first if you want to keep any information.

## Saving Changes

The Settings page has a **Save bar** at the bottom of the form sections:

- Shows "You have unsaved changes" when fields have been modified
- Shows "All changes saved" when everything is persisted
- Click the **Save Changes** button to persist profile changes (name, username, bio, profile picture)

Some settings save immediately without the Save button:
- Theme toggle (light/dark)
- Email notifications toggle
- Default portfolio selection
