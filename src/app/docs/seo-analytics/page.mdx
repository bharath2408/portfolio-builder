# SEO & Analytics

Foliocraft helps your portfolio get discovered by search engines and lets you track how visitors interact with it.

## SEO Settings

Search Engine Optimization (SEO) controls how your portfolio appears in Google, Bing, and other search results, as well as when shared on social media.

### Accessing SEO Settings

1. Open your portfolio in the Studio Editor
2. Click the **SEO** button in the top bar (magnifying glass icon)
3. The SEO panel opens with three fields

### SEO Fields

#### SEO Title
The title shown in search engine results and browser tabs.

- **Where it appears:** Google search results headline, browser tab title, social media share cards
- **Recommended length:** 50–60 characters
- **Best practice:** Include your name and role
- **Example:** `Alex Chen — Full-Stack Developer & Designer`

#### SEO Description
The meta description shown below the title in search results.

- **Where it appears:** Google search result snippet, social media share description
- **Recommended length:** 150–160 characters
- **Best practice:** Summarize what visitors will find, highlight your unique value
- **Example:** `Full-stack developer with 5+ years of experience building React and Node.js applications. View my projects, skills, and get in touch.`

#### OG Image
The image shown when your portfolio is shared on social media platforms (Facebook, Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Discord, Slack).

- **Recommended size:** 1200 x 630 pixels
- **Supported formats:** PNG, JPG, WebP
- **Best practice:** Use a professional headshot, a screenshot of your portfolio hero section, or a custom branded graphic
- **Upload:** Click to upload via Cloudinary

> **Tip:** If you don't set an OG image, social platforms may auto-select a random image from your page or show nothing — always set one for professional sharing.

### SEO Best Practices

1. **Be specific** — "Alex Chen — React Developer in SF" is better than "My Portfolio"
2. **Front-load keywords** — put the most important words first in the title
3. **Write for humans** — descriptions should be compelling, not keyword-stuffed
4. **Unique per portfolio** — if you have multiple portfolios, give each unique SEO settings
5. **Update regularly** — keep your description current with your latest work

## Analytics

Foliocraft tracks visitor analytics for each published portfolio, giving you insight into who's viewing your work.

### What's Tracked

Every visit to a published portfolio records:

- **View count** — total number of page loads
- **Device type** — whether the visitor is on desktop, mobile, or tablet
- **Referrer** — where the visitor came from (Google, LinkedIn, Twitter, direct, etc.)
- **Timestamp** — exact date and time of each visit

### Viewing Analytics

1. Go to **Portfolios** in your dashboard sidebar
2. Find the portfolio you want to analyze
3. Click the **chart icon** (bar chart) on the portfolio card
4. The analytics page opens with your data

### Analytics Dashboard

The analytics page shows:

- **Total Views** — lifetime view count with a trend indicator
- **Views Over Time** — a chart showing daily/weekly view patterns
- **Device Breakdown** — pie chart of desktop vs. mobile vs. tablet visits
- **Top Referrers** — where your traffic comes from, ranked by count
- **Recent Views** — a timeline of the latest visits

### Understanding Your Data

- **Spikes in views** — may indicate your portfolio was shared on social media or referenced in a job application
- **Mobile vs. desktop** — if most visitors are on mobile, prioritize mobile-responsive design
- **Referrers** — if most traffic comes from LinkedIn, optimize your portfolio for professional audiences
- **Time patterns** — if views happen during business hours, recruiters might be viewing your portfolio

## Contact Form Submissions

If your portfolio includes a **Contact Form** block, visitor submissions are automatically collected and stored.

### Viewing Submissions

1. Go to **Portfolios** in your dashboard
2. Click the portfolio with the contact form
3. Navigate to the **Submissions** tab
4. See all entries in chronological order

### Submission Data

Each submission includes:
- **Name** — the visitor's name
- **Email** — their email address
- **Message** — their message content
- **Timestamp** — when the submission was made

### Tips for Contact Forms

- **Keep it short** — name, email, and message are usually enough. Don't add unnecessary fields
- **Use clear placeholders** — help visitors understand what to enter
- **Test it yourself** — submit a test entry to verify it works and appears in the Submissions page
- **Check regularly** — don't miss potential clients or opportunities
